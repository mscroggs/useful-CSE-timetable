Granular Media and the Effect of Grain Shape on Dynamics
Robert P. Lipton
Monday, February 27 1:50 PM–2:05 PM
This is the 1st talk in Nonlocal Models in Computational Science and Engineering - Part II of II (1:50 PM–3:30 PM)
E108

We examine the effect of grain shape on the dynamics of granular media. Grain shape influences the dynamics of hopper flow, the interaction between vehicle tire and gravel road, avalanches, mudslides, etc. In this talk we discuss a model for mesoscopic modeling of the granular flow that is focused on grain shape and strength. The dynamics is represented by an ode in terms of nonlocal constitutive laws relating force to deformation. Here the length scale of nonlocality is taken on the particle length scale and is smaller than the mesoscopic length scale characterizing the flow. We provide a method for solving these odes and several simulations of road bed dynamics are provided. This is in collaboration with Debdeep Bhattacharya at LSU.

A Nonlocal Model for the Numerical Solution to Richards’ Equation
Sabrina F Pellegrino
Monday, February 27 2:10 PM–2:25 PM
This is the 2nd talk in Nonlocal Models in Computational Science and Engineering - Part II of II (1:50 PM–3:30 PM)
E108

We present a nonlocal, derivative free model for transient flow in unsaturated, heterogeneous, and anisotropic soils based on the peridynamic formulation of continuum mechanics. In the proposed model, we consider a spectral method based on the implementation of Chebyshev polynomials to approximate numerically the solution of the model. We show the convergence of the method and present several simulations to study the properties of the solutions.
