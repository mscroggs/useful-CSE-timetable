We present a novel method based on Spiking Neural Networks for the construction of numerical integrators of ordinary (ODE) and partial differential equations (PDEs). For illustration purposes, we provide applications to a host of ODEs and PDEs of variable complexity.

Towards Optoelectronic p-bits and p-bytes Using CMOS Compatible Single Photon Avalanche Diodes (SPADs)
Luke Theogarajan
Friday, March 3 11:50 AM–12:05 AM
This is the 2nd talk in Codesigning a Probabilistic Computing Future: Applications and Algorithms - Part II of II (11:30 AM–1:10 PM)
G104

Optoelectronic devices open up a new avenue for probabilistic computing. Single-Photon Avalanche Diodes (SPADs) are a particularly exciting since they can be fabricated in an unmodified CMOS process. While SPADs have been used as true random number generators, they generally require additional digital circuitry and offer only a modest improvement over current approaches. In this talk, I will present a new approach exploiting the physics of SPADs to enable probabilistic neurons. We leverage the photon statistics to enable the stochastic behavior of the probabilistic bit. We make the event rate dependent on the computational energy by using the intrinsic filtering in the circuit and the event is converted into a state (p-bit) using a latch. I will then discuss how the event based nature can be further exploited to represent multi-valued states (or p-bytes). Results from a proof-of-principle SPAD based Ising and Potts annealers using discrete SPAD arrays show the realization of the concept in hardware along with the potential of using light intensity to control the global computational temperature. I will then conclude the talk by discussing some future avenues of exploration.
