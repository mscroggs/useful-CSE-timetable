Building Your Professional Network
Wil Schilders
Wednesday, March 1 4:00 PM–4:15 PM
This is the 1st talk in Life Skills for Research -- Growing in your Career (4:00 PM–5:40 PM)
E108

Networking is one of the most important ingredients of a successful career. Both in academia and in industry, knowing people and people knowing you is extremely important. An extensive professional network will stimulate collaborations, it will lead to joint projects, jointly organized conferences, invited presentations, increased number of citations, and much more. Especially in this day and age, social media are part of building a professional network, with LinkedIn probably the most suitable. Social media can boost your career if used in the right manner. In this presentation, I will show how I experienced building a professional network, what the advantages were for me. There will also be some general advice on how to build a professional network and how to use it for growing your career.

Planning and Managing Research Projects
Michael A. Heroux
Wednesday, March 1 4:20 PM–4:35 PM
This is the 2nd talk in Life Skills for Research -- Growing in your Career (4:00 PM–5:40 PM)
E108

Planning and managing research projects are key aspects of work in computational science and engineering (CSE), necessary for students as well as early-career, mid-career, and senior professionals who may work in academia, national labs, and industry. This presentation will discuss strategies for planning and managing multidisciplinary CSE research, from the perspectives of individuals, research teams, and teams of teams (that is, aggregate teams who provide complementary expertise in CSE research collaborations). We will consider issues related to planning and managing work overall, as well as issues specific to research software, which encapsulates expertise across applied mathematics, computer science, and domains of science and engineering to enable sustained CSE collaboration and scientific progress.

Communicating Your Science: Increasing your Influence and Impact
Jeffrey A. Hittinger
Wednesday, March 1 4:40 PM–4:55 PM
This is the 3rd talk in Life Skills for Research -- Growing in your Career (4:00 PM–5:40 PM)
E108

You have great technical ideas and results, but how do you turn these into new grants, a thriving research program, and a successful research career? Of course, we would hope the strength and logic of our ideas would speak for themselves, but that is seldom the case in practice. Often, the challenge is in what and how the ideas are communicated. In this session, we will explore how to engage with different audiences, such as researchers not in your discipline, proposal reviewers, sponsors, and the public. We will discuss a variety of ways to help capture and hold your audienceâ€™s interest and to ensure that you communicate effectively.
